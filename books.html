Cruel Education Series

Book 1 · Cruel Education

Merciless

Kit Webb won a full scholarship to Blackthorne College. The catch was buried deep in the pages: one year of servitude to founding family heir Rafe Ashford.

He's beautiful and dangerous. She's brilliant and trapped. The dynamic is simple: he controls everything. Her time, her body, her dignity. Public humiliations filmed and shared. Private degradations no one sees. All while she maintains top marks in engineering — because that's what he wants.

The genius who kneels. The prodigy who scrubs floors. Intelligence made subordinate to power.

Kit can endure cruelty. What she can't endure is her own response when he touches her gently, when his praise feels genuine, when proximity creates confusion she can't afford.

She hates him. She wants him. Both are true.

When Rafe's desperation turns her servitude into something darker, Kit faces an impossible choice. She picks survival.

Ruthless

Every day, Rafe Ashford understands a little more. About powerlessness. About survival. About what it means to lose everything.

I should feel victorious. Instead, I feel complicated.

Because my body still wants him. And watching him break is breaking something in me too.

What happens when revenge stops feeling like revenge?

I don't have the answer. But I'm going to keep punishing him until I figure it out.

Some love stories are written in scars.

Cold and Alive

Her mother's final betrayal was leaving her with them.

The Embry boys: Flint, Cole, and Ash. They are legends for all the wrong reasons. Now, thanks to her mother's whirlwind marriage and abrupt disappearance, they are Jade's jailers. Trapped in their house, she is their new toy: a pet to be tormented, a slave to their whims, a victim for their violence.

But every blow, every humiliation, fuels a fire they can't see burning behind her eyes. Jade has survived worse, and she's learned from it. The Embry boys think they've broken her. They're wrong. She will turn their cruelty into her weapon, and she won't stop until she has taken it all. Everything.

Standalone

The Sin Eater

She heals the damaged. He collects the damned.

Indigo reads bodies the way most people read books. Every scar a sentence, every knot of muscle a confession. When CEO Cyrus hires her to treat his dying body, she discovers a man whose guilt is killing him from the inside out.

He's built an empire on ruin. And in her hands, the monster who controls everything finally surrenders.

But Indigo has her own darkness. A sister she failed to save. A hunger for broken men that has nothing to do with healing and everything to do with atonement.

When therapeutic touch becomes dominance and submission, when she learns exactly what atrocity his body confesses, she should leave.

Instead, she kneels.

Because some men can't be saved. Some darkness can't be cured, only managed. Some women are done pretending they want the light.

A dark romance about the devil who found his absolution and the woman who became his cathedral.

I Hate Everything About You

Ten years ago, Knox Harlow made Briar's life hell. Then she left, rebuilt herself from scratch, and never looked back.

Until her mother got sick and she had no choice.

Now she's back. He's still here. And nothing about him is what she prepared for. Not the way he looks at her, not the way the campus worships him, and definitely not the way her own body keeps betraying her best intentions.

She knows exactly what this is. She is getting the psychology degree to prove it.

Knowing doesn't help.

Dark, explicit, and refusing to apologise for either. For fans of morally complex heroes, heroines with teeth, and romances that earn every page of their happy ending.

The Inheritance Clause

The inheritance has conditions.

Stay one year. Endure whatever Caspian DeVeer decides that means. Watch the payments clear each month and keep my mouth shut.

Simple enough, when the alternative is a six-year-old dying in a hospital bed.

Caspian is cruel, controlled, and carrying scars I wasn't supposed to see. I'm strategic, patient, and running out of reasons to keep hating him as straightforwardly as I'd like.

Three hundred and sixty-five days. A fortune at the end. A brother who gets to live.

I just didn't account for what I'd want to stay for after.

Burst My Bubble

Bubbles has rules: no feelings, no complications, no falling for a man twice her age who looks at her like she hung the stars and makes her feel, for the first time in her life, like she can just be.

She broke one of those rules before. She's been running from the evidence ever since.

Dagger has one rule: wait.

She'll get there. He's sure of it. He's a patient man.

But when a camping trip engineered by their very-not-subtle best friends puts them in unavoidable proximity, patience starts to feel a lot like suffering.
